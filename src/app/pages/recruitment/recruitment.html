Join our team!

Seasonal Hiring

iGEM Guelph is always looking for passionate students to join our team. Every season, we hire new teammates to contribute to our innovative projects and help advance the field of synthetic biology.
Members are hired for a specific team but will collaborate with other teams through the completion of multi-disciplinary projects and by taking on additional roles such as Human Practices Representative. This is a great opportunity to hone and broaden your technical skills, work within an interdisciplinary team, and expand your professional network. Learn more about the open positions and apply below! Contact us at igem@uoguelph.ca if you have any questions.

WET LAB

Modelling Team Member Computational Modelling of our project and data analysis.

Modelling Team Members contribute to the completion of the modelling project. The Modelling team develops mathematical and computational models to support the Wet Lab team by predicting, characterizing, and explaining the behaviour of genetic systems. Models may be mathematical or computational representations of gene expression, protein function, and cell, system, or process behaviours depending on the needs of the Wet Lab team.

Core Responsibilities

  • Complete tasks as assigned by the Modelling Team Lead and Director of Wet Lab.
  • Maintain open communications with the Modelling Team Lead.
  • Attend and actively participate in all relevant meetings including modelling and team-wide meetings.
  • Collaborate with Software to achieve project goals.
  • Manage the organization of departmental documents and resources.
  • Attend and actively participate in all relevant meetings including executive, PI, departmental, and team-wide meetings.
  • Complete deliverables including presentations, administrative forms, and wiki pages.

Required Qualifications

  • Foundational understanding of modelling methods and tools relevant to synthetic biology.
  • Willingness to learn and collaborate within an interdisciplinary team.
  • Interest in modelling and synthetic biology.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Strong understanding of modelling methods and experience using modelling tools relevant to synthetic biology.
  • Experience working with: Python, MATLAB, R

Time Commitment

  • 5-10 hours per week

ENGINEERING

Director of Engineering Managing hardware team activities.

The Director of Engineering is responsible for maintaining hardware team activities including funding and resource procurement, communications external to the Dry Lab team, project timelines, project definition, and team management. They must have experience with the design process and excel in leadership and project management.

Core Responsibilities

  • Define and execute hardware projects in collaboration with other teams.
  • Manage communications and partnerships with UofG and external organizations and procure funding, materials, equipment, services, and online tools as required to complete engineering activities.
  • Manage the Hardware team, resolve team conflicts, facilitate inter-team communication and collaboration, organize meetings, and delegate tasks.
  • Create and enforce project specific timelines and ensure deliverables meet iGEM standards and requirements.

Required Qualifications

  • Experience in leadership roles and project management.
  • Strong oral and written communication skills.
  • Team player with strong interpersonal skills.
  • Strong understanding of the design process and willingness to learn.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Experience in a laboratory setting.
  • Knowledge of and/or experience with microbiology, optics/light systems, vertical farming, biosensing, and/or food processing.

Time Commitment

  • 10-20 hours/week

Media Team Member Manage visual and digital communication.

The Media Team manages all visual and digital communication for iGEM Guelph. They ensure the team's work is clearly, creatively, and accurately represented across platforms. This position is ideal for graphic designers, video editors, illustrators, content creators, photographers, and anyone with strong aesthetic/visual communication skills.

Core Responsibilities

  • Manage and update all social media platforms.
  • Design graphics, posters, and promotional materials.
  • Create videos and illustrated content explaining the project.
  • Capture photos and videos at team events, outreach activities, and lab work.
  • Collaborate closely with the Marketing/Business team for branding consistency.
  • Support other teams (Events, Education, Wet Lab) with media needs.
  • Attend bi-weekly team meetings and outreach-planning meetings.

Required Qualifications

  • Ability to meet weekly deadlines and communicate consistently.
  • Basic experience with at least one of the following:
    • Graphic design
    • Video editing
    • Illustration
    • Videography/Photography
  • Comfortable using design tools (e.g., Canva, Adobe Suite, Procreate, etc.).
  • Strong attention to detail and creativity.
  • Ability to work collaboratively with the Marketing/Business, Events and Education team.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Experience planning or coordinating events (school clubs, residence life, fundraisers, workshops, etc.).
  • Familiarity with budget planning or working with finance teams.
  • Creative idea generation for event themes and engagement strategies.
  • Experience working with community partners or campus organizations.
  • Strong problem-solving skills especially during fast-paced event execution.

Time Commitment

  • 2-5 hours/week, plus additional time for major events, deadlines, etc.
